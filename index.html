Brief Career Journey#

Hi, I am Apurve. I’m a Data Scientist from India.

In early 2014, during my undergraduate at Thapar University I was introduced to a technique called gradient descent optimization. Working on solving for the weights while reducing the error function for a toy regression problem made me realize the potential of a relatively simple gradient optimization method to find the equations that represent a real world model. It combined my adoration of calculus with a creation of a model that represented a real world interaction.

Admittedly at that time I was oblivious to the literature of the cohorts of Machine Learning algorithms already existing in the world and what constitutes a causal relationship. What did favour me was the timing, as this was the time when Deep Learning in the form of very large Neural Networks, was showing immense potential in Computer Vision. Already adept at Python, through the remainder of my undergrad I managed to catch up to the various aspects of Machine Learning and Deep Learning, and was ready to start applying it not only to Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing but wherever possible.

I scoured the job landscape to look for a Data Science internship but they were none to be found in India in 2015-16. So I started working as a Data Engineer intern at a fintech startup with a potential for some Data Science as well, while continuing to polish my ML skills.

Moving on from that and after picking up a few projects I joined Netomi where I helped build the machine learning pipeline for customer service chat and email bots. Till June 2020 I was working as a Data Scientist at Housing. Becoming disillusioned with the lack of rigorous Data Science work there, I started to work as a freelancer, using this as a way to become a savant at delivering my end to end projects. Since then I have been working on relatively short term projects of a wide breadth in Data Science and Python web development. After doing that for a while I am ready to go back to working on long term projects in a high stakes environment with whip smart people.
