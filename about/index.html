About Me


Hi, I am Apurve. I’m a Data Scientist and a Machine Learning Engineer.

Currently, I am working as the Principal Data Scientist at DriveBuddyAI, which is the AI and telematics arm of Roadzen.


  • DriveBuddyAI Dec 2022 - present

    DriveBuddyAI provides a driver and fleet safety product.

    This product detects vehicle and driver behavior risks. This ADAS system provides real time AI based behavioral alerts to drivers, tracking and reporting to fleet managers. My role as a Data Scientist is building and improving the alert, tracking and analytics pipelines.

  • Freelancer 2020 - 2022

    In 2020 some people reached out to me to work on a couple of interesting ML engineering problems. These were founders of early to mid stage startups. I decided this was a good time to work directly with the stakeholder, while being involved in the all the aspects of the problem.

    This included building a Transformer based NLP model to get answers to financial questions from recurring company filing reports. These were mostly related to Climate Change disclosures by organisations used for investment decision making.

    I also worked on building data pipelines for a hotel group booking platform, a feature store for ML tasks and many other projects.

    This phase of work involved a lot of interesting projects but not many of them gave me the opportunity to go deeply into the core of the problems. So I decided to move back to working for one organisation full time.

  • Housing.com Nov 2019 - June 2020

    This was a bigger organisation with stores of data from their real-estate marketplace. I worked on a variety of projects in a small span of time.
    • Creating readable textual descriptions of apartments from tabular data using GPT-2 based model.
    • Deployed an improved version of image auto-filter for the rental listings that contain object detection models.
    • A house rent price suggestion for brokers.

  • Netomi July 2017 - Oct 2019

    Netomi provides conversational AI platform for various clients.

    We built the NLP pipeline that gave the required information from the input texts. My main focus was to built the intent classification model, smaller elements of text processing pipeline and the deployment. I created the pipeline to support various clients such as T-mobile, Signtel, Target, Westjet and many others.
    Over the course of more than two years I iterated over various of text classifiers that would require low domain level training data and would give very high precision score. This included RNN based, graph based and various techniques. Eventually I used a LLM that could be finetuned with small datasets to build the conversation pipelines with very low false positives.
    At that moment at least our definition of the Intent/text classification problem seemed like a “solved problem”.
